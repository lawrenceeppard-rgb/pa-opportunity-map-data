Building a Healthy Media Diet

Curating trustworthy sources for accurate, unbiased information

Why It Matters

It is very important to maintain a healthy media diet that offers an accurate account of the news of the day with limited bias. Information literacy is the foundation of informed citizenship.

Start Simple

Consider choosing a few outlets from our "Trustworthy Sources" list and sticking to those sources each week. This allows you to be a well-informed citizen getting a balanced view.

Gold Standards

The Associated Press and Reuters are excellent examples of trustworthy journalism. Consuming news from these and similar sources creates a very healthy media diet.

Our Trustworthiness Rubric

NewsGuard Standards

  • Not publishing false content
  • Responsible news gathering
  • Making corrections/clarifications
  • News/opinion differentiation
  • Avoiding deceptive headlines

Ad Fontes Reliability

  • Cannot fall below 32.0 on vertical reliability axis

Partisanship Check

  • Cannot be rated hyperpartisan by Ad Fontes or Connors Institute

Recent Record

  • No serious missteps in accuracy or bias in last 12 months according to Connors Institute analysts

We consider using all of these quality guardrails in conjunction to be akin to a "Swiss cheese defense." While one organization could make a mistake, it is highly unlikely that all would give an unreliable outlet high marks.
